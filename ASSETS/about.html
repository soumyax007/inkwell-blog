About this blog

Hello, I'm Soumya.

I am a first-year computer science student with a habit of noticing things and a need to write them down somewhere. This blog is that somewhere. I write about whatever I am thinking about — which is usually design, code, books, or the particular texture of traveling without a plan.

I started Inkwell because I was tired of keeping a private journal that no one ever pushed back on. Writing in public is uncomfortable in a useful way. It forces you to actually finish a thought instead of trailing off into a vague feeling.

I post when I have something worth saying. Some months that is four times. Some months it is zero. I think that is fine.

What I work on

During the day I study and build small web things. I am interested in the overlap between visual design and front-end development — the part of the stack where how something looks and how it works are the same question.

I have also been spending time with data structures and algorithms lately, mostly because they are hard and I do not like leaving things hard. Outside of computers: I read fiction slowly, cook badly, and go on long walks with no destination.

Why “Inkwell”?

An inkwell is where you dip a pen before writing — a reservoir, not the writing itself. The name felt right for a blog that is more about accumulation and process than finished products. I am not trying to publish articles. I am trying to think out loud.

"A writer only begins a book. A reader finishes it." — Samuel Johnson
