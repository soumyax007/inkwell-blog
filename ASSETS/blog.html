Why Constraints Make Better Designers

Having infinite options is the enemy of good decisions. The best work I have ever done came from a brief with only three sentences and a tight deadline. When you cannot rely on tools, templates, or time, you have to rely on ideas. That is where design actually lives.

A blank canvas is not freedom — it is paralysis with good lighting.

I spent a summer working at a tiny studio that had one license for one design application and a shared laptop that overheated. We shipped three projects that quarter. None of them looked like they came from a budget-constrained team. They looked like they came from people who had to think.

Code

CSS Grid Is Still Underused

Most developers I know reach for Flexbox before they have even finished reading the design file. And most of the time it works — sort of. You get the columns, you get the spacing, but you also get a cascade of nested divs and a layout that breaks on exactly one screen size you forgot to test.

CSS Grid was designed for two-dimensional layouts. That sounds obvious, but it changes how you think. Instead of stacking flex containers, you describe the whole layout up front. The browser handles placement. Your HTML gets flatter. Your CSS gets shorter. Your sleep gets longer.

Books

Reading Slowly on Purpose

At some point in the last few years I started tracking how many books I read per year, which immediately turned reading into a sport I was losing. Speed matters in sports. In books it mostly just means you remember less.

I tried an experiment last winter: I gave myself one book for the whole month of December. Chekhov's collected stories. I took notes in the margins, looked up words I skipped past, reread paragraphs that did something unusual. I finished eleven stories. I can still tell you what each one was about.

Travel

Spiti Valley Without a Plan

We decided to go to Spiti Valley eleven days before we left. No hotel bookings. No confirmed return route. A shared backpack between two people and the understanding that we would figure things out. We got to Kaza at 2 AM with the temperature at minus four and a guest house owner who woke up only after we knocked for ten minutes. He made us tea. We stayed for four days.

The roads in Spiti are the kind that remind you a road is a recent invention. The mountains are not interested in you. That indifference is, somehow, the whole point.

Productivity

The Problem With Productivity Advice

Every productivity system I have ever tried was invented by someone who wakes up at 5 AM voluntarily. I do not wake up at 5 AM voluntarily. I have tried. The outcome is just a worse version of me who is also tired.

The deeper issue is that most productivity advice is really lifestyle advice in disguise. It is telling you who to be, not how to get things done. What actually works is embarrassingly personal and usually not worth writing a book about.
